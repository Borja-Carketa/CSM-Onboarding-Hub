What This Means for Your Role

You are not a product expert. You are a Customer Success Manager. Your job is to make sure agents and agency principals on your portfolio are actually using VelociFI — logging in, running territory searches, pulling rate presentations, and managing their book of business.

Understanding the value chain tells you where the pain is. An agent who cannot pull rates is losing deals. An agent not tracking territory is flying blind. Your conversations should be diagnostic — understanding where they are in the cycle and removing whatever is in the way.

For engaged accounts, help them get AmpliFI installed at active dealerships. An agent with AmpliFI at multiple stores is embedded in those stores in a way that creates strong retention on both sides.

The CSM Playbook

How we actually work — the habits, workflows, and standards that make a great CSM at Carketa. Click any section to expand.

The Consultant Mindset

Know the product in and out — every button, every feature, every screen. That mastery is the price of entry, not the job itself. You learn the product cold so the customer never has to.

The goal is never to teach the customer what each individual button does. The goal is to understand what they need and get them to value right away. Their needs lead every conversation; the features are just how you deliver on them.

We do not run checklists. A CSM who walks a customer through a list of features is a tour guide. A CSM who diagnoses where the customer is trying to go and uses the product to get them there is a consultant. Be the consultant.

Never reach out to a customer cold. Before any call, email, or text, pull the full picture from every system. This takes ten minutes and it is the difference between a generic touchpoint and a conversation that moves the account forward.

  • 1
    Gmail — Search the customer's name and domain. Read the recent threads, not just the last one.
  • 2
    RingCentral texts — Texts arrive in Gmail as notifications. Search from:service@ringcentral.com plus the customer's phone number to pull the full text history.
  • 3
    Fireflies — Search by company name or participant email. Read the summaries and action items from past calls. Were commitments made? Were they kept?
  • 4
    HubSpot — Open the company page. Read the notes, check the ticket stage, and review logged activity.
The test: if the customer mentioned something on your last call and you ask about it on this one, you build trust. If you ask a question they already answered, you lose it.

Every customer call gets closed out the same day with the same four steps. An undocumented call did not happen.

  • 1
    Pull the Fireflies recording — Grab the summary and action items while the call is fresh.
  • 2
    Send the recap email — Short recap to the customer: what was covered, what they committed to, what you committed to, and the next scheduled touchpoint.
  • 3
    Log it in HubSpot — Log the call at the company page with structured notes: attendees, topics, action items, and next steps.
  • 4
    Update the ticket stage — Move the customer's ticket in the VelociFI F&I Pipeline to the correct stage. If the account's reality changed on this call, the stage changes the same day.
Recaps are retention. A customer who receives a recap after every call knows they're being managed by a professional. It also creates a paper trail that protects everyone when “you never told us that” comes up.

A standard first onboarding session runs 30–45 minutes. This agenda is a frame, not a script. Open by understanding what the customer needs, then let those needs decide where you spend the time. The goal is not to demo every feature — it's to get the customer to do the workflow that matters most to them, with their own hands, before the call ends.

  • 1
    Intro and context (5 min) — Their agency, their territory, their Admins, what they want out of the platform.
  • 2
    Territory Insights walkthrough — Run a real search in their territory with their criteria. Show them the INV % / SOLD % read.
  • 3
    First rate pull (if applicable) — Open a real dealer from their results, click RATES, pull from their Admin, download the PDF.
  • 4
    One route — Build one route from the map so they leave knowing how field days work.
  • 5
    Mobile app — Make sure it's installed before the call ends. It's listed as “Carketa” on iOS and Android.
  • 6
    Close with next steps — Set the next check-in date on the call. Never end without a scheduled next touchpoint.
Success metric: the customer accomplished the thing they came for — with their own hands — and left knowing exactly how the platform serves their specific book. If you did all the clicking, or you covered every feature but never touched their actual need, the onboarding didn't land.

VelociFI Onboarding & Expansion Playbook

The account lifecycle from kickoff to expansion — phases, exit criteria, and the revenue motions built into every touchpoint.

Purpose

Onboarding is complete when the customer is getting enough value from VelociFI that introducing AmpliFI to their dealers, adding users, or expanding territory is a natural next step rather than a sales ask. Every account exits onboarding with three things answered: dealer outcome confirmed, AmpliFI conversation had, expansion signals assessed.

The Three Phases
Phase 1: Setup CompleteTarget: Day 7
Exit Criteria
  • 1Kickoff/training call completed (Fireflies record exists)
  • 2Territory configured and confirmed correct with the customer
  • 3Every licensed user has logged in at least once (verify in PostHog; the account-level license means dormant seats are invisible unless checked)
Failure trigger: No training call completed by Day 14 → escalation outreach from CSM. Do not let the account sit quietly.
Phase 2: ActivatedTarget: Day 30
Exit Criteria
  • 1Find: Customer has run dealer prospecting searches on their own, outside of training calls
  • 2Pitch: Customer has generated pitch material or reports for at least 5 dealers
  • 3Usage rhythm: 2 or more active days per week, sustained for two consecutive weeks
Failure trigger: No self-driven usage by Day 30 → schedule a re-onboarding call, not a routine check-in. If still stalled at Day 45, move the ticket to Risk stage.
Phase 3: GraduatedTarget: Day 45 to 60
Exit Criteria
  • 1Usage is stable or growing week over week with no support dependency
  • 2Dealer outcome confirmed. Ask directly on a check-in call: “Has VelociFI gotten you in front of a dealer yet: meeting booked, pitch delivered, or contract signed?” A yes is the real graduation signal. A customer logging in daily but with no dealer conversations landed is not ready for any expansion ask.
  • 3AmpliFI conversation had (see below)
  • 4Expansion signals assessed (see below)
The AmpliFI Bridge

Every graduation call includes the question: “Which of your dealers would be a fit for AmpliFI?” This is a standard part of the lifecycle, not an opportunistic upsell. Even a “none yet” answer is logged.

The funnel after graduation:

Graduated → AmpliFI conversation had → First dealer introduction made → Dealer onboarded to AmpliFI

Track each step separately so conversion rates are visible.

Framing: AmpliFI is positioned around the customer's interest, not ours. Their dealers process contracts faster, which makes the agent stickier with those dealers. If you cannot articulate why the introduction benefits the customer, the signal is not ripe yet.

Preferred Partner accounts: AmpliFI activation is already part of their onboarding, so the question shifts from “would you introduce us?” to “which dealers are we activating first?”

Expansion Signals
State Expansion Signals
  • 1
    Territory saturation: the customer has pitched a high percentage of available dealers in their licensed states. Strongest predictor. Get into the conversation before the plateau shows up as declining usage.
  • 2
    Border behavior: searches or expressed interest in dealers just outside their licensed territory
  • 3
    Tier position: customers at the top of their current state tier have a low-cost marginal expansion story
User Expansion Signals
  • 1
    Usage concentration: one power user carrying an account with multiple licensed people is both a fragility risk and an adoption ceiling
  • 2
    Hiring signals: new agents or reps appearing in emails, calls, or meeting transcripts. A new hire at the agency triggers a “let's get them set up” touch within the week.
Before Every Call

The Standing Three-Question Block

Every graduation call and every check-in after includes:

1. “Has VelociFI gotten you in front of a dealer yet?” (or for graduated accounts: “Any new dealer wins?”)

2. “Which of your dealers would be a fit for AmpliFI?”

3. “How's coverage in your current states, and any hiring planned this quarter?”

Three questions, about ninety seconds. Every account touch scans for all three revenue motions.

Pacing Rules

Always Scanning, Rarely Asking, Never Twice in a Row

  • 1
    Questions are diagnostic, not pitches. When a signal fires, note it and decide separately whether to act. “We're hiring two reps” gets “send them my way, I'll get them set up,” not a quote.
  • 2
    One motion per conversation, maximum. If the AmpliFI conversation is live, expansion waits for the next touchpoint. No signal decays in three weeks.
  • 3
    Signals trigger asks, calendars do not. No signal means no conversation. An account with no saturation, no hiring, and no dealer wins just gets good service.
  • 4
    Value confirmed before any ask. The dealer-outcome criterion must be met before pitching AmpliFI, states, or seats. Expanding a tool that has not paid for itself yet reads as pushy.
  • 5
    Frame everything as their math. Surface their growth problem with our solution attached: “You've pitched 80% of the dealers in your territory” is about their pipeline, not our revenue.
  • 6
    Let them open the door when possible. The questions are designed to create that opening. “We're running thin on prospects in Texas” is the customer starting the expansion conversation themselves.
  • 7
    60-day cooldown. Any account that has heard an expansion or AmpliFI ask in the last 60 days is off-limits for another, regardless of signals. A declined ask means log the objection and revisit next quarter, not next call.
